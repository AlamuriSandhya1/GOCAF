About the Programme
What is Project CARE?

Project CARE is a structured animal recovery support program for community animals that need more than emergency response — they need rehabilitation. We support animals that:

Have suffered injury or accidents

Hit by vehicles, attacked, or otherwise hurt and unable to recover on their own.

Need post-surgery monitoring & care

Recovery support after sterilization or other medical procedures.

Have aggression or trauma issues

Animals shaped by past abuse or fear that need patient behavioral work.

Need behavioral rehabilitation

Trust-building, socialization, and gradual preparation for safe release.

Need temporary shelter & return protocol

Short-term safe space, full monitoring, and structured return-to-area planning.

The Need

Why Rehabilitation Matters (Just Like Humans)

  • Injured / traumatized animals need a safe space
  • Stressed bodies and wounds need rest
  • Behavioral healing requires time
  • Some need slow integration before release
  • Some require continuous medication & monitoring
Eligibility

🐾 When Animals Need CARE

  • Hit by vehicle / road accidents
  • Severe injury or wound recovery
  • Surgery recovery (post-ABC)
  • Old or weak strays
  • Disease recovery (skin conditions, infections)
  • Behavior modification needed
  • Special diet & care required

The CARE Journey
What We Do — From Intake to Safe Return

Five carefully designed stages, every animal moved through with patience, dignity, and skill.

1
Day 1: Safe Intake

Animal arrives at the CARE facility. Initial checkup, intake recorded, profile created in the SAVE system.

2
🛏️ Step 2: Recovery Environment

Calm, clean, low-stress space prepared — the foundation of healing.

3
Step 3: Rehabilitation & Care

Daily medical treatment, nutrition, behavioral work, and supervised recovery activities.

4
Step 4: Observation & Stability

Continuous monitoring of physical health and behavioral stability before any release decision.

5
Step 5: Safe Return

Animal returned to its original territory, caregiver informed, post-release tracking continues via SAVE.
